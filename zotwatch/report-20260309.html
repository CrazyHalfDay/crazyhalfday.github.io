Genesis of copper-deficient skarn iron deposits in the North China Craton: Insights from apatite and whole-rock geochemistry

2026-03-04

Jun-Wu Zhang，Lin Li，Ju-Quan Zhang，Fang-Yue Wang，Xian Liang

DOI: 10.1016/j.gexplo.2026.108030

We collected apatite major element and whole rocks major and trace element data from skarn Fe/Cu deposit-associated and barren intermediate–acid plutons in both regions. These indicators provide a practical tool for identifying Cu-fertile plutons within the North China Craton. Based on these findings, comparative analysis suggests that the copper deficiency in intrusions of North China Craton's skarn iron ore clusters results from lower La/Yb, ε Nd (t), and AST values, and higher oxygen fugacity. This implies two probable causative mechanisms: first, derivation from a magma source inherently depleted in copper, which is unfavorable for copper mineralization; second, the high oxygen fugacity likely dispersed copper within the iron-dominated ore system, preventing the effective concentration and formation of discrete copper orebodies. This study provides significant new insights for assessing Fe Cu fertility of plutons.

Geology (IF: 4.8)

DOI: 10.1130/g53970.1

Reducing conditions are typical of incipient serpentinization in which sulfides and alloys break down to form secondary phases. Sulfides and alloys are the main hosts of platinum group elements (PGEs) in serpentinites, and the role of serpentinization in the remobilization of these elements is controversial. Here, we replicated early serpentinization using cold-seal pressure vessels, reacting a mixture of olivine, synthetic laurite, and water at 50 MPa and temperatures of 250−350 °C for durations of 1−4 d. Silicate mineral assemblages and breakdown of primary laurite are consistent with observations in natural and experimental systems where incipient serpentinization occurred at highly reducing conditions. The Chah-Musa copper deposit, located within the Cenozoic Toroud-Chah Shirin (TCSH) magmatic arc in northeastern Iran, represents an example of porphyry-epithermal mineralization in the region. This study aims to investigate the local geology, mineralogical and geochemical characteristics, wall-rock alteration, fluid inclusion properties, and geological context of the Chah-Musa deposit. The deposit is hosted by a subvolcanic diorite porphyry intrusion that intruded into Eocene volcano-sedimentary sequences consisting of volcanic breccia, agglomerate, and red tuffaceous rocks. In summary, the Chah-Musa deposit documents a prolonged magmatic-hydrothermal evolution that reflects fluid and metal transport processes within an evolving porphyry-epithermal system, thereby contributing valuable insights into the metallogenic potential of the TCSH arc.

The Cambrian explosion marks a key period in Earth’s evolution, with the first appearance of nearly all known modern metazoan phyla and associated pronounced Earth system changes. The nickel−molybdenum (Ni−Mo) sulfide-rich polymetallic black shales in the 521 Ma Niutitang Formation in the South China blockare unparalleled worldwide and therefore of significance for economic geology and Earth system science research. However, their formation regimes and broader implications are not fully understood. Based on high-resolution petrological and geochemical analyses, combined with available data sets, we found that the ocean shifted from oxygen-rich to hydrogen sulfide−rich (euxinic) between Cambrian Age 2 and Age 3, conditions which, along with hydrothermal metal input, promoted the formation of metal sulfides. Hydrothermally sourced Ni precipitated directly, whereas Mo was concentrated via seawater circulation and redeposition in euxinic seawater. The formation and burial of organic matter and sulfides enhanced net oxygen release to the atmosphere, contributing to the subsequent shallow-ocean oxygenation and further setting the stage for the third (main) phase of the Cambrian explosion. Our findings highlight the interplay of hydrothermalism, ocean redox evolution, and biological innovation during the Cambrian explosion, and explain the uniqueness of these mineralized shales with different patterns in that key period.

Tectono-magmatic evolution of the central Kohistan Arc, NW Pakistan: granitoid emplacement revealed by mineral chemistry

2026-03-07
Journal of Asian Earth Sciences (IF: 2.4)

DOI: 10.1016/j.jseaes.2026.107049

The central Kohistan Arc consists of Late Cretaceous to Early Tertiary, subduction-related, calc-alkaline to high-K granitoids with varied textures and compositions, reflecting several stages of a complex tectono-magmatic evolution. In this study, we present major element data of amphibole, feldspar, and biotite, along with trace element data of zircon from seven plutons, previously grouped into two categories: (1) pre-50 Ma intra-arc subduction-related and (2) post-50 Ma arc-continent collision-related granitoids. Amphibole compositions indicate that the intra-arc-related granitoids (Matiltan granite and Gabral quartz diorite) were less hydrous (∼4.6–5.8 wt% H 2 O) and crystallized at lower temperatures (740–745 °C) and pressures (3.0–3.6 kbar) corresponding to shallow mid-crustal depth, as well as under reducing conditions (logfO2: −17 to −15). The biotite and zircon compositions consistently reflect a calc-alkaline subduction-related origin for all plutons. Zircon Eu-anomalies additionally suggest a progressive deepening of the magma sources from ca. 40 to 65 km depth, consistent with a change of the tectonic regime from arc-related to collision-related magmatism accompanied by crustal thickening and assimilation.

The Careón Ophiolite is one of the best-preserved Devonian supra-subduction zone ophiolites (c. 395 Ma) exposed along the Variscan Orogen. The mantle section is comprised of harzburgites and metasomatic dunites/olivine-rich harzburgites. Dunites and olivine-rich harzburgites acted as focused conduits for melt transport through the main harzburgite section. The circulation and reaction of the melt through pre-existing harzburgite imposed major modal metasomatism, consisting of incongruent orthopyroxene dissolution, which produced major whole-rock SiO 2 -depletion and minor MgO-enrichment. Harzburgites and dunites/olivine-rich harzburgites show similar low-depletion in the less incompatible immobile elements, such as whole-rock Al 2 O 3 , TiO 2 , V, HREE, as well as in Cr#-Mg#-TiO 2 compositions in the primary unreacted Mg-Al-rich spinel. This low-depletion is interpreted as a residue of moderate melting (10–18%) after partial melting by decompression in a MORB ridge without significant external H 2 O-input. Two types of cryptic refertilized REE patterns are identified based on LREE and HREE fractionations. The less-fractionated type is characteristic of the interaction between peridotite and MORB-like melts, whereas the more-fractionated type is related to the interaction with H 2 O-rich highly LREE-enriched IAT-like melts. The same partial melting throughout the entire mantle section, the existence of two distinct refertilization events and their spatial–compositional relationship with the Devonian IAT mafic crust, suggest that the mantle of the Careón Ophiolite was located in a Devonian forearc. This Devonian forearc developed above an older Cambrian oceanic back-arc lithosphere preserved at the margin of Gondwana. The relative stability of olivine compared to orthopyroxene during serpentinization, produced highly-serpentinized harzburgites and slightly-serpentinized dunites/olivine-rich harzburgites, indicating relatively high-temperature serpentinization. The composition of the serpentinization fluids and the low-temperature re-equilibration of pyroxenes determine the whole-rock CaO-depletion in harzburgites relative to dunites/olivine-rich harzburgites, which constitutes the major metasomatic change during serpentinization.

研究背景：TTG（英云闪长岩-奥长花岗岩-花岗闪长岩）是太古宙大陆地壳的重要组成部分，其源区是否混入经海底热液蚀变的洋壳物质，直接关系到早期地球是否存在类似现代俯冲的物质循环机制。该研究的动机在于，尽管锆石O、Si同位素被认为是追踪TTG源区组成的有效工具，但此前缺乏针对不同太古宙潜在源岩部分熔融后同位素响应的定量约束，因此难以将观测到的同位素信号与具体岩石类型一一对应。

方法详情：作者选取了巴伯顿地区3.45 Ga和3.22 Ga的TTG样品，系统测定其全岩及锆石的氧同位素和硅同位素组成，以同时追踪低温海底蚀变和硅质交代过程留下的地球化学指纹。研究的关键方法不仅是获取实测同位素数据，还将这些数据与数值模型耦合，模拟不同候选源岩（如普通镁铁质岩石、受海底热液作用改造的镁铁质岩石、硅化海底岩石）在部分熔融过程中熔体及结晶锆石的O-Si同位素演化。模型进一步预测不同源区混合比例下TTG母岩浆和锆石的同位素范围，再与巴伯顿样品的观测值进行匹配，从而反演源区中海底蚀变/硅化岩石的贡献比例。通过联合分析全岩与锆石数据，研究尽量避免仅依赖单一同位素体系可能带来的多解性，提高了对源区性质判别的可靠性。

研究结果：研究表明，巴伯顿TTG的全岩和锆石O、Si同位素组成不能仅由未蚀变的镁铁质源岩部分熔融解释，而更符合源区混入了显著比例海底热液硅化镁铁质岩石的情形。定量模型显示，这类来自海底的硅化镁铁质岩石在TTG源区中的贡献最高可达约30 wt. %，说明太古宙TTG形成时并非单纯熔融“原始玄武质下地壳”，而可能涉及已经在海底经历低温-热液改造的物质。其重要意义在于，这为太古宙早期存在海底蚀变岩石向深部搬运并参与熔融提供了直接地球化学证据。结合区域地质背景，作者据此提出巴伯顿地区在古太古代至少局部存在类似俯冲的过程，能够将硅化海底岩石埋入TTG源区，从而支持早期地球已有一定程度板块样式地球动力学活动的观点。

局限性：首先，该研究的解释较大程度依赖数值模型对源岩类型、熔融条件及同位素分馏行为的设定，而太古宙实际源区可能比模型假设更复杂，因此混合比例和过程判别仍存在一定不确定性。其次，研究对象主要集中于巴伯顿地区的两个时代TTG样品，区域代表性较强但全球可推广性尚需更多克拉通实例验证。再次，类似的O-Si同位素异常在理论上也可能受到后期变质、源区非均一性或多阶段熔融-混染过程影响，因此“俯冲样埋藏”虽然是当前最优解释，但未必是唯一解释。

未来方向：后续研究可将O-Si同位素与Hf、Ti、Li、B等锆石或全岩指标联合起来，在更多太古宙克拉通中检验硅化海底岩石参与TTG成因的普遍性。进一步开展更精细的高温实验和熔融-结晶分馏模型，也有助于更准确约束不同海底蚀变岩石在部分熔融过程中对锆石同位素的具体控制机制。

DOI: 10.1016/j.chemgeo.2026.123337

Large-scale fault systems in continental collision zones channel deep fluid fluxes and facilitate crust-mantle interactions, yet the processes governing spatial geochemical heterogeneity of hydrothermal fluids within them remain poorly constrained. This study combines hydrochemical, multi-isotopic, and self-organizing map (SOM) analyses of geothermal gases and waters to clarify fluid origins and evolution mechanisms along the eastern Tibetan Plateau's Xianshuihe (XSHF)-Anninghe-Zemuhe (AZF)-Xiaojiang (XJF) fault system. The results reveal systematic geochemical variations: (1) The XSHF zone exhibits CO 2 enrichment derived from inorganic carbonate dissolution, metamorphic decarbonation, and mantle contributions. Elevated 3 He/ 4 He ratios (0.26–2.67 Ra) indicate significant crust-mantle mixing. Geothermal waters are predominantly of the Na-HCO 3 type, with an average reservoir temperature of 206 °C, consistent with deep hydrothermal alteration of silicate minerals. (2) The AZF-XJF system is characterized by N 2 -rich gases, predominantly from atmospheric and crustal organic sources. Combined He-C isotopes suggest a crustal origin of CO 2 , influenced by organic sediments. Hydrochemical types are diverse (Ca-HCO 3 , Ca-SO 4 ), reflecting carbonate rock dissolution and shallower fluid circulation. Geophysical data reveal that these geochemical patterns are structurally controlled: the XSHF is underlain by mantle melts and crust-mantle low-resistivity zones that facilitate mantle-derived fluid ascent, enhanced by high slip rates. In contrast, the AZF-XJF system exhibits high-resistivity crustal structures that inhibit mantle upwelling, with low fault activity and permeability further limiting deep fluid release. This study demonstrates deep structural architecture controls hydrothermal fluid sources and pathways, advancing our understanding of tectono-fluid coupling in continental collision settings.
