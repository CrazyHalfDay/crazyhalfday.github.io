Carbon cycling during crustal magmatism: Constraints from Fe-Mg isotopes of Early Cretaceous adakitic rocks in Liaodong Peninsula, NE China

2026-01-23
Geological Society of America Bulletin (IF: 3.9)

Yikang Quan，Jiaru Sheng，Haochen Duan，Wei Zhang，Yan Jing 等

DOI: 10.1130/b38747.1

Carbon cycling mediated by magmatism is fundamental to planetary habitability, yet the role of crustal magmatism, particularly adakitic rocks, in tracing deep carbon transfer remains poorly constrained. Here, we employ coupled Fe-Mg isotopes in Early Cretaceous low-Mg (LMA) and high-Mg adakitic rocks (HMA) from northeastern China to identify the geochemical fingerprint of carbon cycling within crustal magmatism. The LMA exhibit high δ56Fe (0.14‰ to 0.23‰) and low δ26Mg (−0.40‰ to −0.28‰), whereas HMA display low δ56Fe (0.05‰ to 0.15‰) and high δ26Mg (−0.31‰ to −0.21‰). Partial melting modeling and major-element variations indicate that they originated from a carbonated eclogite source. The widespread distribution of Mesozoic adakitic rocks across northeastern China demonstrates that carbonated eclogite is a critical carbon reservoir. Temporal correlations between regional Mg isotope compilations and subducted carbon fluxes reveal that slab subduction (Paleo-Pacific plate in Mesozoic; Pacific plate in Cenozoic) facilitated carbon recycling. Our results establish adakitic rocks as robust tracers of deep carbon cycling and demonstrate that plate-tectonic processes have governed this transfer over millions of years, advancing understanding of Earth’s deep carbon cycle and its planetary habitability implications.

East Meets West: The Trace of the Mesoproterozoic Kibaran Event in the Mantle Lithosphere Beneath Eastern Tanzania

东西交汇：坦桑尼亚东部地幔岩石圈中中元古代基巴拉事件的遗迹

2026-01-22
Journal of Geophysical Research: Solid Earth (IF: 3.9)

Sheng‐Hua Zhou，Qiao Shu，Sally A. Gibson，Hong‐Fu Zhang，Jing‐Jing Zhu 等

DOI: 10.1029/2025jb032555

Abstract We present elemental geochemistry and multiple isotopic systematics (Re‐Os, Lu‐Hf, Sm‐Nd and Sr) for mantle peridotite xenoliths from Lashaine in northern Tanzania. We use the data to examine how the major Proterozoic tectono‐thermal events that affected the crust of the western Tanzanian craton are imprinted on the lithospheric mantle in the Mozambique belt adjacent to the eastern margin of the Tanzanian craton. Whole‐rock and mineral compositions together with 187 Os/ 188 Os ratios of Lashaine peridotites are consistent with Archean‐aged cratonic mantle assembled to create the root beneath the Tanzanian cratonic nucleus. Highly radiogenic 87 Sr/ 86 Sr ratios (0.70411−0.83604), unradiogenic Nd (ɛNd = −14 − +2) and variable Hf isotope ratios (ɛHf = +4 − +2,912) of minerals combined with the other geochemical features in Lashaine peridotites reflect extensive melt extraction followed by metasomatic enrichment of the lithospheric mantle by subduction‐related melts/fluids. Mineral Lu‐Hf isotopic compositions define a 1.4 Ga isochron and, together with the distinctive, robust Lu‐Hf model age (1.4 Ga) of one garnet with very high 176 Lu/ 177 Hf ratio (3.182), indicate that a major Mesoproterozoic subduction‐related metasomatic event reset Lu‐Hf isotope systematics of the Lashaine peridotites. This over‐printing of the mantle lithosphere indicates a clear link to the Mesoproterozoic Kibaran event along the western margin of the Tanzanian craton. We invoke a flat‐slab subduction model during the Kibaran event to introduce subduction‐related components in a pervasive Mesoproterozoic metasomatic event beneath Lashaine.
