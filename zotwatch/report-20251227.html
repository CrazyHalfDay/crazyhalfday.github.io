Magnetotelluric Imaging of a Magma Plumbing System From the Asthenosphere to Crust Beneath the Newer Volcanic Province, Australia

澳大利亚新生代火山区从软流圈到地壳的岩浆通道系统的大地电磁成像

2025-12-26
Geophysical Research Letters (IF: 4.6)

Xinpeng Ma，Yunhe Liu，Changchun Yin，Haibin Yang，Zhihao Rong 等

DOI: 10.1029/2025gl118189

Abstract The Newer Volcanic Province (NVP) in southeastern Australia is a classic example of continental intraplate volcanism located far from a plate boundary, yet the configuration of its magma plumbing system remains unclear. Using three‐dimensional (3D) magnetotelluric (MT) inversion, we imaged a vertically continuous, trans‐lithospheric conductive structure beneath the NVP. This structure consists of a sub‐horizontal zone in the asthenospheric mantle, interpreted as 0.1–1.0 vol.% carbonate‐rich melt, vertically linked to hydrous basaltic melt zones (2–5 vol.%) in the lithospheric mantle and shallow crust. These upper crust conductive zones likely represent remnant melt or metasomatic minerals. These features define a vertically integrated magma plumbing system from the asthenosphere to the surface. The geometry and depth of the anomalies are consistent with scenarios in which the carbonate‐rich melt may form in response to shear‐driven upwelling and edge‐driven convection. These observations provide new constraints on possible mantle processes that drive intraplate volcanism.

研究背景：The Newer Volcanic Province (NVP) is a textbook case of continental intraplate volcanism far from active plate boundaries, yet its deep magma supply network has remained enigmatic. Understanding how melt is generated and transported through >100 km of lithosphere is essential for deciphering the engine of such isolated volcanic activity.

方法详情：The authors collected a dense array of broadband and long-period magnetotelluric (MT) stations across the NVP and performed rigorous 3-D inversion to recover electrical resistivity from the surface to ~250 km depth. Anisotropic and topography-cognizant forward modelling were incorporated to minimise artefacts, while iterative sensitivity tests constrained the minimum melt fraction required to explain observed conductors. The final resistivity model was jointly interpreted with published seismic tomography and geochemical data to discriminate melt from metasomatic phases.

研究结果：A vertically continuous, sub-vertical low-resistivity anomaly (<10 Ωm) links a carbonate-rich asthenospheric melt layer (0.1–1.0 vol.%) at ~110 km to hydrous basaltic reservoirs (2–5 vol.%) in the lithospheric mantle and upper crust beneath the NVP. The geometry supports shear-driven upwelling and edge-driven convection as viable mechanisms for intraplate melt generation, and implies that the NVP is fed by a trans-lithospheric magma plumbing system rather than by isolated pockets. These findings provide the first geophysical image of a fully connected melt pathway from asthenosphere to surface in southeastern Australia.

局限性：MT resolution decreases with depth, so the exact thickness and lateral continuity of the carbonate-rich layer remain uncertain. The inferred melt fractions depend on assumed carbonatite–basalt resistivity relationships, which can vary with volatile content and temperature; trade-offs with graphite or sulfides are not uniquely ruled out.

未来方向：Higher-resolution ocean-bottom MT and joint inversion with seismic anisotropy could sharpen the deep mantle flow pattern, while targeted noble-gas geochemistry of the youngest NVP cones may test the carbonate-rich source hypothesis.

研究相关性：Researchers investigating intraplate volcanism, lithosphere–asthenosphere interaction, or electrical-resistivity signatures of mantle melts will find this paper a key reference for imaging methodologies and for comparative studies in other continental settings.

Turbulence and Mixing in Deep‐Water Stratified Tidal Bottom Boundary Layers

深水层化潮汐底边界层中的湍流与混合

2025-12-26
Journal of Geophysical Research: Oceans (IF: 3.3)

Madelaine G. Rosevear，Gregory N. Ivey，Nicole L. Jones，Bishakhdatta Gayen，Matthew D. Rayson

DOI: 10.1029/2025jc022931

Abstract Ocean currents flowing over the seabed produce a turbulent bottom boundary layer (BBL) which extracts energy from the overlying flow and mediates sedimentary, chemical, and biological processes at the seafloor. The role of the BBL is especially important in relatively shallow shelf seas where stratification, mean flow, and tidally‐driven oscillating currents are typically strong. Despite this importance, the dynamics and even the height of the BBL formed under stratified, tidal conditions are not well understood. We address this knowledge gap by performing large‐eddy simulations of the evolution of a tidally forced and stratified BBL, initialized from rest, over a range of geophysically relevant parameter values. The BBL has two distinct regions: an actively stirred, near‐well‐mixed bottom mixed layer (BML) and a weakly stirred and strongly stratified capping pycnocline. A simple steady‐state empirical model, dependent on stratification, tidal period, and bottom turbulence intensity, provides a useful estimate of BML height. Motivated by appreciable growth in the BML that can occur on timescales of days to weeks, we also develop a time‐dependent model for the BML height. This model accounts for the vertical structure of the BBL turbulence and reproduces the BML growth with high fidelity. We also present a simple model for the cycle‐averaged eddy diffusivity, which depends on the BML height. Both the steady‐state and time‐dependent models of BML height—and the accompanying expression for eddy diffusivity—can be used to develop tidal BBL parameterizations and inform grid resolution requirements within ocean models, as well as guiding sampling for future observational studies.

A Data-Driven Integration of Pore Geometry and Aspect Ratio into Rock Physics Models for Porosity Partitioning and Elastic Property Estimation in Carbonate Reservoirs

将孔隙几何与纵横比数据驱动地整合进岩石物理模型以实现碳酸盐岩储层孔隙划分与弹性参数估算

2025-12-25
Geophysics (IF: 3.0)

Eliane Petersohn，Guilherme Pena Castilho，Tapan Mukerji

DOI: 10.1190/geo-2025-0480

Abstract Carbonate reservoirs exhibit complex, heterogeneous pore systems, with pore shapes and sizes spanning nanoscale to meter scale due to primary and secondary porosity, varied fabrics, and textures. Conventional rock physics models, effective for structurally consistent clastic reservoirs, rely on generalized pore geometry assumptions that often fail to capture carbonate variability and therefore require calibration. A data-driven approach enhances Differential Effective Medium (DEM) models for carbonate reservoir characterization by integrating empirical pore system data. A robust real-world dataset comprising over 1,750 thin sections from five wells—one of the largest reported for carbonate reservoirs—provided the foundation for image analysis to quantify pore-type volume fractions and aspect ratios, directly informing the model. Analysis revealed that reference pores (interparticle porosity) dominate, with crack-like pores comprising less than 0.1% of the total pore population, challenging typical model assumptions. The pore geometry data were incorporated into rock physics models through optimization techniques, establishing a valuable aspect ratio database for carbonates. Unlike conventional approaches that rely on predefined pore categories (reference, crack, stiff) and treat aspect ratios as fitting parameters or constant values assigned to each pore type within a target layer, aspect ratios were derived directly from measured data, enabling precise porosity partitioning. These insights supported evaluation of elastic property variability, quantification of pore geometry’s impact on compressional and shear velocity predictions, and assessment of pore type distribution across the reservoir. Optimizations based on compressional velocity alone, shear velocity alone, and both velocities jointly yielded R2 values exceeding 90%, indicating high predictive accuracy. The approach also revealed scale-dependent fracturing effects, refining reservoir characterization. This method addresses conventional model limitations, improving porosity partitioning and elastic property estimation in complex carbonate reservoirs while providing a framework applicable to future studies.
