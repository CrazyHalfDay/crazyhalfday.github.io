2026-02-27

DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-68646-3

Abstract Subduction of carbon rich sediments and crust at convergent plate boundaries exerts a crucial control on Earth’s mantle chemistry and surface habitability. Recent attention has focused on exposures of fully-carbonated mantle rocks as these may attest to an overlooked sink for subducted carbon not sampled by arc volcanism. However, even in the best-studied example, the Semail Ophiolite, Oman, the setting for carbonation remains highly contentious, with conflicting inferences from geochemistry and geochronology. We approach this problem by combining microanalysis of halogens and detailed petrography to fingerprint the origins of carbonating fluids. Fluids were derived from both sedimentary pore fluid expulsion and deep slab decarbonation reactions in a subduction zone setting. Through mass balance modelling we show that CO 2 fluxes into the forearc from deep decarbonation (1.7–3.4 × 10 13 gyr −1 C) could represent up to 90% of the global flux entering subduction zones, indicating that carbonated mantle peridotites likely represent a major sink for subducted CO 2 which may have varied through geological time.

Residual volcanic activity and deep degassing at Massico-Roccamonfina system (central and southern Italy): New insights from fluid geochemistry

2026-02-22
Journal of Volcanology and Geothermal Research (IF: 2.3)

Degassing processes in quiescent volcanic areas provide valuable tools to monitor deep magmatic and tectonic activities. Near Roccamonfina volcanic complex, at the boundary between the Campania-Lazio regions in central-southern Italy, two main degassing areas were identified, suggesting (i) residual volcanic activity and/or (ii) the presence of active faults enabling deep fluids to mix with shallow groundwaters. A comprehensive hydrogeochemical survey was carried collecting waters and gases from deep and shallow wells, springs, and natural gas vents. The investigation aimed to (i) characterize the chemical composition of circulating fluids, (ii) delineate areas influenced by deep degassing, (iii) understanding the origin of the collected fluids and (iv) providing a comprehensive model of the current volcanic/tectonic activity in the area. Our dataset integrates measurements of major ions, dissolved gases, stable isotopes (δ 18 O/δD/δ 13 C-TDIC), and helium isotopic ratios (R/Ra). Results reveal a progressive southward increase in mantle-derived helium, fixing the transition between Roman and Campanian magmatic provinces. Significantly, R/Ra values up to 1.87 in the northeastern caldera sector of the Campanian Plain indicate a significant, if compared to volcanic areas more south (e.g. Campi Flegrei), deep contribution, linked to ongoing regional dynamics. Evidences of deep degassing were also detected in areas recently affected by seismic swarms. The spatial distribution of dissolved gases and isotopic signatures: CO₂, N₂, δ 13 C-TDIC, and R/Ra, points to fluid ascent along regional-fault-controlled pathways. These findings highlight the complex processes and exchanges between (i) fluid circulation, (ii) crustal permeability and (iii) tectono-volcanic activity within this active segment of the Italian volcanic belt.

Deep seismic tremor reveals trans-crustal fluid dynamics beneath Tengchong Volcano Field, Southwest China

深部地震颤动揭示中国西南腾冲火山场下方的穿地壳流体动力学

2026-02-24

The architecture and dynamics of trans-crustal magmatic systems dictate their surface expression. While well-studied at subduction zones, rifts, and hotspots, intraplate volcanoes distal from such tectonic boundaries remain poorly understood. Here, we report the discovery of persistent deep tremor in the intraplate Tengchong Volcanic Field. Analysis of a 12-year seismic dataset reveals narrowband tremor (0.4–2.0 Hz) occurring in swarm-like episodes lasting up to 24 h. Tremor sources are localised at 40–50 km depth in the lower crust/uppermost mantle, coincident with a multi-disciplinary imaged deep magma reservoir. Between 2014 and 2020, the source region migrated upward ~10 km, while the dominant frequency systematically increased; these shifts correlate with changes in tremor amplitude and source scattering. We interpret these observations as evidence for episodic fluid-pulse ascent from the deep reservoir. This tremor provides a novel seismic probe into the active energy flux within an intraplate trans-crustal system far from tectonic boundaries.

Accretion of volatile elements on Earth without the need of a late veneer

地球上挥发性元素的吸积无需晚期增生

2026-02-25
Science Advances (IF: 11.7)

Lucas Calvo，Julien Siebert，Dongyang Huang，Ingrid Blanchard，Edith Kubik 等

DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.ady8018

Volatile elements are essential for life development and planetary evolution. However, the timing of their delivery to terrestrial planets remains unclear. Sulfur, selenium, and tellurium are volatiles, but also siderophile elements. Their abundances in Earth’s mantle can be used to determine whether volatile elements were delivered to Earth during or after the segregation of the core. Here, we experimentally measured their partition coefficients between core-forming metal and mantle silicate under pressure, temperature, and oxygen fugacity conditions relevant to a deep magma ocean. Our results show that these elements exhibit similar partitioning behaviors, indicating that core-mantle equilibrium preserves their chondritic relative abundances. If a volatile-rich late veneer has been delivered to Earth after core segregation, it must have been limited in mass, making up a maximum of 0.1% Earth’s mass. This suggests that volatile elements, including water, were accreted continuously during Earth’s growth rather than being delivered predominantly by a late veneer of volatile-rich material such as carbonaceous chondrites.

Growing concerns about plastic pollution and climate change worldwide are driving research into the effects of microplastics on carbon cycling.
