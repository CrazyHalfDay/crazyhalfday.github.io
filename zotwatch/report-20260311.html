Diffusive copper redistribution from shallow degassed magmas to mafic enclaves. Insight from anomalously Cu-enriched enclaves from Bezymianny volcano, Kamchatka

铜从浅部脱气岩浆向镁铁质包体的扩散再分配——来自堪察加 Bezymianny 火山异常富铜包体的启示

2026-03-09
Journal of Volcanology and Geothermal Research (IF: 2.3)

Vesta O. Davydova，Vasily D. Shcherbakov，Pavel Yu. Plechov，Vasily O. Yapaskurt，Yuri D. Shcherbakov 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.jvolgeores.2026.108588

Mafic enclaves enriched in sulfides and copper compared to their host magmas are found in felsic plutons associated with porphyry deposits and in eruptive products of arc volcanoes. The origin of these Cu-rich enclaves is usually linked to either intrinsically Cu-rich parental magmas or to extensive fractionation of primitive magmas under sulfide-undersaturated conditions before they are stored in shallow felsic reservoirs. Using Bezymianny volcano as a case study, we suggest an alternative mechanism for the formation of Cu-rich mafic enclaves. Detailed petrographic analysis shows that sulfides in Bezymianny enclaves evolve from scarce early Cu-poor MSS (chilled monosulfide solid solution close to pyrrhotite or euhedral pyrrhotite with thin ISS rims) enclosed in phenocrysts, to Cu-rich ISS (intermediate solid solution close to cubanite) in matrix glass and vesicles. This increase in Cu content with crystallization requires the external addition of Cu. Mineral oxybarometry indicates that the shallow-stored magma (<1 kbar) had an oxygen fugacity of 0 to +1 log units above NNO. Intense degassing of S-bearing aqueous fluid kept sulfur concentrations in silicate melts below sulfide saturation, destabilizing sulfides and causing incompatible behavior of chalcophile elements. Consequently, Cu accumulated in silicate melt until inputs of sulfide-bearing mafic magma from deeper levels (9–2 kbar) triggered its diffusive redistribution into enclaves. Cu diffused from the Cu-rich, sulfide-free silicate melt of the shallow reservoir into the Cu-poor silicate melt of mafic enclaves, and subsequently into their sulfide phases. High diffusion rates of monovalent Cu allow small enclaves to achieve full Cu enrichment within several weeks.

研究背景：这项研究聚焦于弧火山和与斑岩矿床有关的长英质岩浆系统中常见的一类现象：镁铁质包体相对于寄主岩浆异常富集硫化物和铜。传统解释通常认为这类包体要么源自本身富铜的原始岩浆，要么来自在硫化物未饱和条件下经历强烈分异的深部基性岩浆；而本文以堪察加半岛的别济米扬尼火山为例，提出一种新的成因机制，即浅部脱气长英质岩浆中的铜通过扩散重新分配到后期注入的镁铁质包体中。研究动机在于解释为何一些浅部火山系统中的包体能表现出异常高铜含量，以及这一过程如何联系岩浆脱气、氧逸度、硫化物稳定性和金属迁移。

研究结果：研究发现，别济米扬尼火山镁铁质包体中的硫化物演化具有明显的时间序列：早期硫化物稀少且贫铜，主要以冷凝形成的MSS或具有薄ISS边的自形磁黄铁矿形式存在；而随着结晶演化，晚期硫化物在基质玻璃和气孔中表现为明显富铜的ISS。这种从早期贫铜到晚期富铜的变化难以仅用包体自身结晶分异解释，反而指示有外部铜输入。作者进一步认为，浅部长英质岩浆在接近NNO到NNO+1的氧逸度条件下发生强烈含硫流体脱气，使硅酸盐熔体中的硫始终低于硫化物饱和，从而导致硫化物不稳定、亲铜元素表现为不相容并在熔体中累积。随后，来自深部的含硫化物镁铁质岩浆注入浅部储库后，铜从浅部富铜但无硫化物的寄主熔体扩散进入贫铜的包体熔体，再进一步进入包体硫化物，最终形成异常富铜包体。其意义在于，这为火山弧岩浆系统中铜的局部富集提供了一个不同于“原始岩浆富铜”或“长期未饱和分异”的替代机制，也对理解斑岩成矿系统中的金属预富集与再分配具有启发性。

Do magmatic sill intrusions simply liberate the sulphur of the sedimentary reservoirs?

岩浆岩床侵入是否仅仅释放了沉积储库中的硫？

2026-03-06
Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta (IF: 4.5)

Alban Cheviet，Flavien Choulet，Martine Buatier，Christophe Galerne，Wolfgang Bach 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.gca.2026.02.043

Sedimentary basins are at the interface between deep (lithosphere) and superficial (hydrosphere, atmosphere and biosphere) reservoirs, with the capacity to trap large quantities of volatile elements (e.g. S, C, H, Cl, F) over time through sedimentation and diagenesis. These volatiles can be released abruptly due to contact metamorphism associated to the emplacement of magmatic intrusions in the sediments, further disturbing the geochemical cycles in the atmosphere and biosphere. The interactions between magma and sediments during sill emplacement are complex and include contact metamorphism and contamination processes, but also the assimilation of sediments into the sill. In this study, we examine the impact of magma-sediment interactions on the sulphur cycle. The Ringvent sill in the Guaymas Basin drilled during Expedition IODP 385, is a funnel-shaped sill that emplaced in soft sediments at the subseafloor, representing an exceptional in-situ natural laboratory for studying magma-sediment interactions. The petrological and geochemical study of the sediments, magmatic rocks and magma-sediment mixtures reveals a high concentration of pyrite at the magma-sediment interfaces. Multiple types of pyrite indicate different sulphur-trapping processes occurring during the emplacement of the sill. Trace elements and isotopic (δ 34 S) analyses were used to identify the different sources of sulphur and to better constrain the processes. We demonstrate that the assimilation of sediment and the contamination of porewater in the Ringvent sill enables the formation of these different pyrite types from the time of sill emplacement until to late hydrothermal circulations, which remain still active. Such processes enable the storage of a minimum of 700,000 tonnes of sulphur within the sill, while there is minimal evidence of the release of volatiles in the surrounding area, indicating that, under certain conditions, these systems can function as a significant sulphur sink.

Abstract Magmatically-accreted upper crust along mid-ocean ridges is traditionally considered to consist of lava flows overlying a sheeted-dyke complex. However, how the upper crust is formed at hotspot-influenced ridge segments remains unknown. Using three-dimensional seismic reflection data, here we report images of lava flow layering beneath Axial volcano on the Juan de Fuca Ridge. These lava flow layers dip inward towards the caldera and rift zones, resulting from subsidence caused by magma withdrawal beneath the summit and rift zone extension. Furthermore, our images reveal that the classic, laterally continuous sheeted-dyke complex is absent. Instead, lava flow layers are rotated and brought into direct contact with the magma domain, leading to partial assimilation of these units. Three-dimensional images also show that melt is injected outward along lava flow layers, suggesting that the upper crust at Axial volcano is formed by complex interactions between injected melt sills and lava flow layers.

Abstract Ultrahigh-temperature (UHT) metamorphism, recording extreme crustal thermal regimes, provides pivotal insights into the evolution of ancient orogens. Terranes with UHT metamorphism distributed within the continental fragments of the former Gondwana megacontinent hold critical clues to the processes and dynamics associated with Gondwana’s assembly and evolution. The Kuunga Orogen documents the collision between northern and southern Gondwana, marking the final assembly of the Gondwana megacontinent. However, the evolution of this orogen, especially its eastern section, which is largely covered by the Antarctic ice cap, remains poorly constrained. Based on integrated petrological, phase equilibrium modeling, and petrochronological analyses, we report UHT metamorphism in spinel-bearing pelitic granulites from the glacial moraines in the Grove Mountains, East Antarctica. Phase equilibrium modeling and mineral thermometry reveal peak temperature conditions of ≥900 °C and ~0.7 GPa for pelitic granulites characterized by the assemblage garnet + spinel + K-feldspar + plagioclase + ilmenite ± sillimanite ± rutile ± biotite + melt, with a subsequent near-isobaric cooling path to ~800 °C. Petrochronological analyses of multiple dateable phases were conducted to constrain the timing of the UHT metamorphism. Zircon and monazite U–Pb dating constrain peak UHT metamorphism to predate ~535 Ma, with cooling to the solidus by ~520 Ma. Biotite/K-feldspar Rb-Sr isochron ages of ~456–440 Ma are interpreted to represent the post-peak cooling stage at ~300–400 °C. Combined with existing regional data, our new results define a two-stage cooling history: an initial rapid cooling at ~18 °C/Myr from ~535 to 500 Ma, followed by a significantly slower stage at &lt; 3 °C/Myr from ~500 to 440 Ma. The UHT metamorphism recorded in the moraine samples indicates that extreme thermal perturbations may have facilitated crustal evolution and maturation of the subglacial orogen in East Antarctica. These findings shed new light on the geodynamic evolution of the eastern Kuunga Orogen and the assembly history of Gondwana.
