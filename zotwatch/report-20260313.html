铜从浅部脱气岩浆向镁铁质包体的扩散再分配——来自堪察加 Bezymianny 火山异常富铜包体的启示

2026-03-09
Journal of Volcanology and Geothermal Research (IF: 2.3)

Vesta O. Davydova，Vasily D. Shcherbakov，Pavel Yu. Plechov，Vasily O. Yapaskurt，Yuri D. Shcherbakov 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.jvolgeores.2026.108588

Mafic enclaves enriched in sulfides and copper compared to their host magmas are found in felsic plutons associated with porphyry deposits and in eruptive products of arc volcanoes. The origin of these Cu-rich enclaves is usually linked to either intrinsically Cu-rich parental magmas or to extensive fractionation of primitive magmas under sulfide-undersaturated conditions before they are stored in shallow felsic reservoirs. Using Bezymianny volcano as a case study, we suggest an alternative mechanism for the formation of Cu-rich mafic enclaves. Detailed petrographic analysis shows that sulfides in Bezymianny enclaves evolve from scarce early Cu-poor MSS (chilled monosulfide solid solution close to pyrrhotite or euhedral pyrrhotite with thin ISS rims) enclosed in phenocrysts, to Cu-rich ISS (intermediate solid solution close to cubanite) in matrix glass and vesicles. This increase in Cu content with crystallization requires the external addition of Cu. Mineral oxybarometry indicates that the shallow-stored magma (<1 kbar) had an oxygen fugacity of 0 to +1 log units above NNO. Intense degassing of S-bearing aqueous fluid kept sulfur concentrations in silicate melts below sulfide saturation, destabilizing sulfides and causing incompatible behavior of chalcophile elements. Consequently, Cu accumulated in silicate melt until inputs of sulfide-bearing mafic magma from deeper levels (9–2 kbar) triggered its diffusive redistribution into enclaves. Cu diffused from the Cu-rich, sulfide-free silicate melt of the shallow reservoir into the Cu-poor silicate melt of mafic enclaves, and subsequently into their sulfide phases. High diffusion rates of monovalent Cu allow small enclaves to achieve full Cu enrichment within several weeks.

研究问题：本文探讨浅部脱气岩浆中富铜基性包体的成因机制。

研究方法：结合岩相学、硫化物演化分析与矿物氧逸度测定开展研究。

主要发现：浅部脱气致硫化物不饱和并富集铜，铜后扩散进入基性包体。

创新点：提出富铜包体可由浅部长英质储库向基性包体的铜扩散形成。

相关性：为弧火山与斑岩成矿中铜迁移、富集和包体成因提供新解释。

研究背景：这项研究聚焦于弧火山和与斑岩矿床有关的长英质岩浆系统中常见的一类现象：镁铁质包体相对于寄主岩浆异常富集硫化物和铜。传统解释通常认为这类包体要么源自本身富铜的原始岩浆，要么来自在硫化物未饱和条件下经历强烈分异的深部基性岩浆；而本文以堪察加半岛的别济米扬尼火山为例，提出一种新的成因机制，即浅部脱气长英质岩浆中的铜通过扩散重新分配到后期注入的镁铁质包体中。研究动机在于解释为何一些浅部火山系统中的包体能表现出异常高铜含量，以及这一过程如何联系岩浆脱气、氧逸度、硫化物稳定性和金属迁移。

方法详情：作者综合使用了详细岩相学观察、硫化物显微结构分析、矿物化学测试和氧逸度估算来重建包体与寄主岩浆之间的金属迁移过程。研究重点放在别济米扬尼火山喷发产物中的镁铁质包体，比较了包体内部不同位置硫化物的形态、组合和铜含量变化，包括早期被斑晶包裹的贫铜单硫化物固溶体（MSS，接近磁黄铁矿）以及晚期出现在基质玻璃和气孔中的富铜中间固溶体（ISS，接近古巴矿成分）。作者还利用矿物氧逸度计推断浅部储库的氧化还原条件，结合压力范围约束浅部储存深度（<1 kbar）与深部镁铁质岩浆来源深度（约9–2 kbar），并据此建立“浅部脱气导致铜在长英质熔体中富集、随后向包体扩散”的成因模型。最后，论文依据一价铜在硅酸盐熔体中的高扩散速率，对小型包体在数周内达到显著富铜的时间尺度进行了论证。

研究结果：研究发现，别济米扬尼火山镁铁质包体中的硫化物演化具有明显的时间序列：早期硫化物稀少且贫铜，主要以冷凝形成的MSS或具有薄ISS边的自形磁黄铁矿形式存在；而随着结晶演化，晚期硫化物在基质玻璃和气孔中表现为明显富铜的ISS。这种从早期贫铜到晚期富铜的变化难以仅用包体自身结晶分异解释，反而指示有外部铜输入。作者进一步认为，浅部长英质岩浆在接近NNO到NNO+1的氧逸度条件下发生强烈含硫流体脱气，使硅酸盐熔体中的硫始终低于硫化物饱和，从而导致硫化物不稳定、亲铜元素表现为不相容并在熔体中累积。随后，来自深部的含硫化物镁铁质岩浆注入浅部储库后，铜从浅部富铜但无硫化物的寄主熔体扩散进入贫铜的包体熔体，再进一步进入包体硫化物，最终形成异常富铜包体。其意义在于，这为火山弧岩浆系统中铜的局部富集提供了一个不同于“原始岩浆富铜”或“长期未饱和分异”的替代机制，也对理解斑岩成矿系统中的金属预富集与再分配具有启发性。

局限性：论文的机制解释建立在别济米扬尼火山这一特定案例之上，因此其普适性仍需在更多弧火山和侵入岩体系中验证。虽然岩相学、矿物化学和扩散时间尺度推断共同支持铜的外部输入与扩散富集，但该模型对初始铜浓度、扩散系数、包体尺寸、温度梯度以及混合作用持续时间等参数较为敏感，仍存在一定解释空间。此外，浅部脱气、硫化物不稳定和铜扩散之间的因果链条主要依据间接证据构建，若缺少更直接的同位素或实验模拟约束，仍难完全排除其他协同过程。

俯冲作用导致新太古代地壳增生岩浆的氧化与水化程度升高（华北克拉通）

2026-03-06
Chemical Geology (IF: 3.6)

Yuan-Yuan Liu，Chao Wang，Mark B. Allen，Hongyu Zhang，Li Su 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.chemgeo.2026.123359

The Neoarchean (2.8–2.5 Ga) represents a critical transition towards a habitable Earth. Gradual development of plate subduction during this era facilitated crustal differentiation and maturation, reflected by compositional changes of the Archean continental crust. However, key physical properties of Neoarchean crust-building felsic magmas, such as oxygen fugacity ( f O 2 ) and water content, remain loosely constrained. Here we show that ca. 2.5 Ga granitoids in the North China Craton are considerably oxidized and hydrous, and comparable to modern arc magmas. Their elevated f O 2 and water content (ΔFMQ −1.0 to 2.8, 5.7–16.6 wt% H 2 O; average at ΔFMQ +0.8 and 9.6 wt% H 2 O) are attributed to fluids released from the subducting oceanic crust, which metasomatized the mantle wedge. Such slab-derived fluids facilitated the generation of Neoarchean oxidized and hydrous felsic magmas through water-fluxed melting of the underplated mafic crust derived from the metasomatized mantle wedge, accompanied by various redox reactions and volatile incorporation. Our work demonstrates that localized synchronous oxidation and extensive fluid circulation across Earth's reservoirs driven by plate subduction likely occurred in some cratons at the end of the Archean.

DOI: 10.1130/b38735.1

Disseminated mineralization is responsible for much of the production from orogenic gold deposits, and deciphering the nature of reactive fluid flow across the macro- to microscopic scale is crucial for understanding the mineralization processes. Compared to siliceous slate, the higher abundance of Fe-bearing minerals in argillaceous slate enhances reactivity at the fluid-mineral interface, promoting sulfide formation and gold precipitation. This study demonstrates that the physicochemical interplay between structural deformation and geochemical reactivity fundamentally controls disseminated mineralization in slate-hosted orogenic gold systems.

We found a special type of fault mirror, tourmaline fault mirrors (TFMs), to be remarkably widespread in the N-S normal fault systems of the Himalayan orogen, these being intermittently yet repeatedly exposed for tens of kilometers along strike, suggesting that the TFMs likely hosted large earthquakes. Here, we report the first field- to nanoscale study of these TFMs from the Mount Everest area. Microstructural analyses show that a typical TFM consists of three distinct layers, from the slip surface to the gneiss host rock: a 30−80-nm-thick film of amorphous tourmaline, a net-textured layer (20−100 µm) containing tourmaline clasts in an amorphous tourmaline matrix, and a dynamically recrystallized layer of tourmaline grains (≤400 µm). The observed amorphous material, micropores, and embayed residual tourmaline grains, combined with Ti-in-quartz thermometry implying temperatures &gt;840 °C at the slip surface, suggest that the TFMs formed by frictional melting during seismic slip on tourmaline-filled veins. Combining unstable frictional slip data for tourmaline gouge at 200−300 °C with the coexistence of tourmaline cataclasites and TFMs, we propose that tourmaline veins play a key role in controlling seismic rupture on the N-S normal fault systems of the Himalayan orogen.
