Ba isotope unmasks recycling processes of fluid mobile elements in subduction zones

钡同位素揭示俯冲带流体活动元素的循环过程

2026-01-27
Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta (IF: 4.5)

Yang Yu，Xiao-Long Huang，Betchaida D. Payot，Hai-Quan Liu，Ying-De Jiang 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.gca.2026.01.038

Geochemical recycling processes in subduction zones exhibit significant variability due to the incorporation of complex subduction components into the mantle wedge. Here, we present whole-rock geochemical and Ba–Sr–Nd–Hf–Pb isotopic data for basalts from the sediment-rich Luzon Arc and the sediment-poor Bicol Arc to constrain the recycling processes of fluid mobile elements (FME) in different subduction settings. Our results suggest that the mantle wedge beneath the Luzon and Bicol arcs was primarily metasomatized by melts from subducted sediments and fluids from subducted oceanic crust, respectively. The basalts from both arcs show variable δ 138/134 Ba values despite their limited variations in Sr–Nd–Hf–Pb isotopes, reflecting the heterogeneous dehydration of phengite in subducted sediments and oceanic crust, which have undergone different extents of Ba isotopic fractionation at shallow depths. Luzon back-arc basalts (BAB) exhibit Sr–Nd–Hf–Pb isotopic compositions similar to those of island arc basalts (IAB), yet possess more variable and lighter Ba isotopic compositions. This defines a distinct Ba isotopic lightening trend across the arc, reflecting progressive Ba isotopic fractionation associated with enhanced phengite dehydration at increasing subduction depths. The Bicol basalts show lower 87 Sr/ 86 Sr and much higher Sr/Zr ratios compared to the Luzon basalts, indicating pervasive fluid infiltration from the subducted oceanic crust into their mantle sources, which promotes the preferential breakdown of lawsonite and results in significant Sr enrichment in the mantle wedge. This observation explains why global arc basalts tend to have higher Sr/Zr ratios with decreasing 87 Sr/ 86 Sr ratios. Notably, Luzon basalts display Ba/Zr, K/Zr, and Rb/Zr ratios that largely overlap with those of Bicol arc basalts. We propose that subducted oceanic crust plays a role similar to that of subducted sediments in recycling FME, such as K, Rb, and Ba, owing to the buffering effect of phengite. Nevertheless, it potentially contributes more significantly to Sr enrichment within the mantle wedge of subduction zones.

Generation of ferroan (“A-type”) granite in a subduction zone setting: Petrology of the Golden Horn batholith, North Cascades, Washington, USA

俯冲带背景下铁质（“A型”）花岗岩的形成：美国华盛顿州北卡斯卡德金角岩基的岩石学

2026-01-27
Geological Society of America Bulletin (IF: 3.9)

Jeffrey H. Tepper，Michael P. Eddy，Samuel E. Berkelhammer，Bruce K. Nelson

DOI: 10.1130/b38681.1

The Eocene Golden Horn batholith (North Cascades, northwestern United States) is an unusual example of coeval ferroan (A-type) and I-type granitoids that formed in a subduction zone setting. Associated mafic to intermediate dikes and stocks (adakites and calc-alkaline basalts to andesites) attest to the presence of mafic magmas in the shallow crust during batholith assembly and imply anomalously hot conditions in the mantle that are attributed to rupture or rollback of the subducting slab. Field, geochemical, and Sr-Nd-Pb isotopic data support generation of the ferroan magmas at midcrustal depth by melting of calc-alkaline plutonic rocks or by protracted differentiation of a parental basalt, and production of the I-type magmas by partial melting of gabbroic rocks at greater depth. Metasomatism by fluids bearing Na, F, and high field strength elements enriched some ferroan rocks to produce peralkaline granite. Mixing between the I-type and ferroan magmas also produced a hornblende-biotite granite with rapakivi feldspars, which is the most voluminous phase of the batholith. Results of this study suggest that ferroan granitoids rarely form in continental arcs because that tectonic regime limits voluminous basalt intrusion into the upper crust, and not because of a lack of suitable source rocks. Their presence in an arc setting likely indicates rollback or tearing of the slab or the presence of a slab window.

Magmatic degassing as the primary source of salt in Archean oceans

岩浆脱气是太古宙海洋盐分的主要来源

2026-01-21
EarthArXiv

Ranta, Eemu，Fusswinkel, Tobias，Beier, Christoph，Bali, Eniko

DOI: 10.31223/X52472

The salinification of Earth’s early oceans impacted both the climate and the evolution of life. However, available halogen data of Archean seawater samples are at apparent odds with a conventionally assumed mantle origin of sea salt, highlighting a critical lack of mechanistic understanding of how the Archean oceans became salty. Here, we present new triple halogen (Cl-Br-I) data from high temperature (~750–840 °C) fluid inclusions from a gabbroic intrusion in Iceland. Our data show that the magmatic degassing fractionates the halogens and generates fluids with high Br/Cl and I/Cl that match with Archean seawater. These observations suggest that seawater salinity in the Archean oceans was primarily regulated via fluids exsolved from cooling intrusions during genesis of early crust, and that the mantle had a modern-like halogen composition by 3.5 Ga. Our findings highlight the strong connection between the chemistry of the mantle, magmatic fluids and the oceans, with implications for understanding the chemical environments that guided the evolution of early marine organisms.

Dislocation creep may control bridgmanite deformation in the Earth’s lower mantle

位错蠕变可能控制地球下地幔中布里奇曼石的变形

2026-01-26
Communications Earth &amp; Environment (IF: 8.1)

Longli Guan，Daisuke Yamazaki，Noriyoshi Tsujino，Yuji Higo，Sho Kakizawa 等

DOI: 10.1038/s43247-026-03212-9

Abstract Seismic anisotropy in the Earth’s lower mantle, particularly around subduction zones, is commonly attributed to deformation by dislocation creep, yet much of the lower mantle appears nearly isotropic. This contrast complicates interpretations of mantle rheology. Here we report the temperature dependence of lattice preferred orientation in bridgmanite, the most abundant lower mantle mineral, through high-pressure deformation experiments at 25 gigapascals and 1700–2100 kelvin. Both iron-free and iron-bearing bridgmanite develop lattice preferred orientations across this temperature range, with distinct slip systems occurring below and above 1800 kelvin. Low-temperature fabric produces strong seismic anisotropy, whereas high-temperature fabric yields weak, near-isotropic signatures under horizontal shearing. These results provide a unified explanation for strong seismic anisotropy near subduction zones and globally near-isotropic behavior of the lower mantle. They suggest that dislocation creep could dominate lower mantle deformation while generating diverse seismic signatures, providing important understanding for lower mantle rheology and dynamics.

Sulfate-driven anaerobic oxidation of methane in deeply buried sediments on the Southern Campbell Plateau

南坎贝尔高原深埋沉积物中硫酸盐驱动的甲烷厌氧氧化

2026-01-27
Chemical Geology (IF: 3.6)

Alex J. Reis，Vanessa Fichtner，Andrea M. Erhardt

DOI: 10.1016/j.chemgeo.2026.123269

Sulfur is a key component in the reduction of organic carbon in marine sediments. Sulfate-driven anaerobic oxidation of methane and microbial sulfate reduction are the leading processes driving the removal of organic carbon and sulfur from the environment. Microbial sulfate reduction has been well studied in most marine depositional environments, however, studies examining sulfate-driven anaerobic oxidation of methane are primarily limited to shallow burial or methane seep environments. Sediments recovered from IODP site U1553 on the southern Campbell Plateau demonstrate evidence of a deeply buried sulfate-methane transition zone (SMTZ). In this study, we identified a deep (> 500mbsf) sulfate-methane transition zone through the analysis of sulfur isotopes in sulfide minerals (d 34 S CRS ). Three potential paleo-SMTZs were also identified as peaks in d 34 S CRS below the modern SMTZ, suggesting the changing seafloor and depositional conditions since the late Oligocene contributed to the upward migration of the SMTZ over time. Understanding the evolution of deep SMTZs and the processes associated with them will help further constrain how the sulfur and carbon cycles interact and drive carbon storage in marine sediments.

The effect of fluorine in the genesis of carbonatite-hosted rare earth element and niobium deposits

氟在碳酸岩型稀土和铌矿床成因中的作用

2026-01-27
Science China Earth Sciences (IF: 5.0)

Wanzhu Zhang，Shuo Xue，Ming-Xing Ling，Xing Ding

DOI: 10.1007/s11430-025-1735-0

Carbonatite is a rare but highly prospective rock that plays a significant role as a source of critical metals, notably rare earth elements (REE) and niobium (Nb). The widespread occurrence of fluorine-bearing minerals (e.g., fluorite, fluorapatite, and bastnäsite) in carbonatites indicates that their parent magmas are rich in fluorine. Nevertheless, the mechanisms by which fluorine controls REE and Nb mineralization remain unclear. Building on previous work, we systematically synthesize the regulatory role of fluorine in REE and Nb mineralization throughout the entire magmatic evolution of carbonatites. Our key findings are: (1) during partial melting, fluorine decreases the solidus of carbonated peridotite, thereby enhancing the generation and stability of carbonatitic melt. (2) In the mantle melting stage, it lowers the partition coefficients of REE and Nb between mantle minerals and melt, effectively promoting their extraction. (3) Throughout magmatic crystallization, fluorine significantly enriches REE and Nb in the residual melt. (4) During liquid immiscibility, fluorine exerts limited influence, as the partition behaviors of REE and Nb are primarily governed by structural differences between the conjugate melts. (5) In the hydrothermal stage, fluorine facilitates the mobilization, transport, and ultimate precipitation of REE- and Nb-bearing minerals, directly driving mineralization. The presented evidence implies that carbonatitic melts and fluoride-bearing minerals exert primary control over the spatial distribution and mineralization potential of REE and Nb. Future studies should focus on (1) clarifying how fluorine promotes REE and Nb enrichment in late-stage brine melts and (2) investigating its effect on the solubility and precipitation mechanisms of REE- and Nb-bearing minerals during the late stages of magma evolution.

3-D velocity model of the crust and uppermost mantle beneath Xinjiang region, constructed by joint tomography of body and surface wave data

基于体波和面波数据联合层析成像构建的新疆地区地壳及上地幔顶部三维速度模型

2026-01-26
Science China Earth Sciences (IF: 5.0)

Zhiqi Zhang，Ying Liu，Huajian Yao，Jikun Feng，Mingshuai Tang 等

DOI: 10.1007/s11430-025-1742-4

The Xinjiang region is situated between the Tethyan and Central Asian orogenic belts. It has undergone multiple episodes of accretionary orogenesis and was tectonically reactivated during the Cenozoic by the far-field compressional effects of the India-Eurasia continental collision. These processes have generated pronounced seismicity and recurrent earthquake-related hazards in this region. A high-resolution three-dimensional velocity model is essential for multi-scale velocity model construction, source-parameter inversion, simulation of strong ground motion, and seismic hazard assessment. In this study, we collected first-arrival body-wave travel-time data recorded by permanent stations in and around Xinjiang and processed both ambient-noise and regional-earthquake surface-wave records. The resulting dataset comprises ∼8.1 million body-wave travel-time picks—including absolute arrival times and event-pair differential times—and ∼5,000 surface-wave dispersion curves (5–50 s period). By joint inversion of these complementary body and surface wave datasets, we determined a high-resolution three-dimensional Vp and Vs model for the crust and uppermost mantle beneath Xinjiang (XJVM-1.0) with a lateral resolution of 50–100 km and a vertical resolution of ∼10 km. XJVM-1.0 reveals that the Tianshan orogen exhibits a relatively rigid upper crust that hosts abundant seismicity under far-field compression, whereas its middle-lower crust accommodates the majority of compressional stress through plastic deformation and/or partial melting. In contrast, the interiors of the Junggar and Tarim basins have experienced negligible internal deformation and are inferred to underthrust beneath the Tianshan orogen in response to the India-Eurasia collision. Relocated seismicity indicates that earthquakes are predominantly concentrated within the Tianshan range, with a notable proportion occurring in the middle-lower crust, implying whole-crust tectonic activity and highlighting the potential for great earthquakes in this region.

Geochemical characteristics, formation age, and mechanism of the cold volcano at Muji travertine, Xinjiang

新疆木吉钙华冷火山的地球化学特征、形成年代及机制

2026-01-26
Science China Earth Sciences (IF: 5.0)

Jiaqi Liu，Ziyang Shen，Bin Zhang，Zhihao Sun，Zeyang Zhu 等

DOI: 10.1007/s11430-025-1766-4
